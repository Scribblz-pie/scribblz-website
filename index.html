SCRIBBLZ

Impeller suction. Custom PCB. Wireless user input.

Our robot is capable of vertical traversal via impeller suction and omniwheels. Attached to its head is a dry-erase marker to doodle a user-input image, communicated wirelessly via this website. Once finished, it returns home to its docking station, controlled by a RaspPi.

The Project

OUR GOAL

To design and build an autonomous robot capable of navigating vertical glass surfaces and creating precise drawings based on user input.

SCRIBBLZ V1 Prototype

1

Suction System

High-powered custom impeller creates negative pressure for reliable adhesion to glass.

2

Wall Driving

Omni-wheel drivetrain enables holonomic motion for precise maneuvering in any direction.

3

Drawing Mechanism

Solenoid-activated marker lift allows for clean lines and complex, disconnected strokes.

4

Auto Docking

Computer vision guided return-to-home sequence for automatic recharging.

Final System

Check out our robot in action! This video shows the final prototype scaling walls, drawing doodles, and returning to its docking station. Watch as SCRIBBLZ brings art to vertical surfaces!
